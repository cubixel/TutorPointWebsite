Development

TutorPoint was born out of the discussion of products that would meet the specification and criteria set by our client. Such discussions gave rise to a variety of candidates for potential projects that CUBIXEL could engage in; suggested ideas included an interactive mobile phone repair manual, a multi-media flash card application, and an art work gallery social media application, just to name a few. Despite a large selection of products, three ideas were favourable amongst team members. Product number one was an interactive PDF-like application specifically designed for practical lab work, offering check-lists, progress bars, and instructional videos. Product number two was an information tracker for the game 'Dungeons & Dragons', designed to help aid the ‘Dungeon Master’ in controlling player stats and locations, also incorporating an embedded digital dice. The third and final product was an online tutoring application incorporating a range of online topics with multimedia abilities such as video chat, text chat, and screen editing with the intent for educational purposes.
